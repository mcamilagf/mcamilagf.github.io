Section 1: GDP and Life Expectancy

Life expectancy and GDP vary significantly across continents. North America and Europe typically enjoy higher GDP and longer life expectancies due to better healthcare and living standards. In contrast, many African countries face lower GDP and shorter life expectancies due to health challenges. Asia presents a mixed picture, with high life expectancy and GDP in countries like Japan and South Korea, while others in South and Southeast Asia have lower figures. Overall, higher GDP is strongly correlated with increased life expectancy, underscoring the influence of economic development on health outcomes.

Section 2: GDP per capita

In 2022, GDP per capita varied significantly among the United Kingdom, United States, China, Congo, Nigeria, and India. The United States had the highest per capita income, followed closely by the United Kingdom. China’s GDP per capita was lower but marked by impressive growth. India’s income was moderate, while Nigeria and the Congo had among the lowest GDP per capita, reflecting ongoing economic and developmental challenges.

Section 5: Inflation since 1800

Since 1800, worldwide inflation has fluctuated due to significant economic, political, and technological changes. The 19th century generally experienced low and stable inflation, except during major events like wars and gold discoveries. The 20th century brought more volatility, with hyperinflation episodes following World War I and II in several countries and the oil crises of the 1970s triggering global inflation surges. Economic stabilization efforts in the late 20th century led to more controlled inflation rates in developed economies. The 21st century has seen periods of both low inflation and deflation, until the COVID-19 pandemic and subsequent supply chain disruptions, alongside geopolitical tensions, drove a resurgence of inflation globally by the early 2020s.

Section 6: Comparing four economies

Kenya's GDP grew steadily due to expanding sectors, while the Congo faced volatility from political and economic challenges. By 2022, the UK and Japan maintained higher GDPs, with Kenya showing consistent growth and the Congo continuing to struggle with economic stability.

Section 7: Unemployment rate in Germany (2000-2022)

Between 2000 and 2022, Germany's unemployment rate fluctuated significantly. It peaked around 11% in 2005 due to economic challenges but steadily declined. By 2019, it reached a low of about 3.2% due to economic growth. The COVID-19 pandemic in 2020 briefly increased unemployment, but government support helped stabilize the rate, which remained low by 2022.

Section 8: Real Weekly Wage in UK (2000-2024)

Real weekly wages in the UK from 2000 to 2024 have fluctuated significantly due to economic conditions and inflation. After steady growth in the early 2000s, wages stagnated following the 2008 financial crisis and struggled to recover in subsequent years. The COVID-19 pandemic further disrupted wage growth, although government support measures helped stabilize incomes. By 2024, while real weekly wages are anticipated to have improved, rising living costs and inflation continue to challenge workers' purchasing power.
